Our custom of bearing the Crest on the Helmet is "borrowed from the ancient fashion of adorning it with some kind of device, to make it appear terrible to the enemy. Helmets are now used as the distinctive marks of Nobility, and are dilgently depicted. They are frequently met with as charges in Coat Armour and are also borne singly as Crests.

The Helmet of a King, or Prince, is full faced,with six bars, all of gold, and lined inside with crimson. P. 24, f. 8.
