BLACKSMITHS, Worshipful Company of (London). (Incorporated 1325.) Sable, a chevron or, between three crowned hammers proper. Crest — On a wreath of the colours, a mount vert, thereon a phoenix with wings endorsed proper firing herself with the sunbeams of the last, and by the agitation and working of her wings she kindleth certain sticks of cinnamon and other spices. Motto — "By hammer and hand all arts do stand." (Ancient motto — "As God wills, so be it.")

[Arms confirmed and crest altered by Sir Wm. Segar, Garter, 24th June
