CAROLINA, Province of (North America). . . . two cornucopia in saltire, mouths upwards . . . Crest — A buck trippant . . . Supporters — (Dexter) an Indian woman holding a baby in her arms, and at her side a small Indian boy holding an arrow; (sinister) an Indian, on his head a crown of feathers and holding a large arrow. Motto — " Domitus scultoribus orbis."

[There is a docket of the above arms in the College of Arms with this note :

"The Arms, Crest and Supporters of the Province of Carolina drawn from the Impression of the Great Seal of that province fix't to the Patent granted to Laurence Cromp, Esqr., late York Herald, to be principal Herald of the said Province, under the hands of his Excellency John, Lord Granville, Palatine, and the Right Honble. the rest of the True and Absolute Proprietors of the said Province, dated the first day of June Anno Dni. 1705."]
