CHATHAM (Kent). Argent, a fesse chequy gules and or, between in chief two ancient ships with three masts and sails proper, colours flying of the second, and in base a sword of the fourth, pommel and hilt of the third surmounted by a trident in saltire and entwined with a wreath of laurel also proper. Crest — Out of a naval crown or, a trident erect, enfiled with a wreath of laurel proper. Motto — " Loyal and true."

Granted August r, 1891.
