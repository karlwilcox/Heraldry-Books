DERRY, See of. Ancient — Argent, a church proper (another, confirmed by D. Mulleneux, Ulster, 24th May 161 3). Gules, three mitres or, the labels argent. Modern — Gules, two swords in saltire proper pommelled and hilted gold, on a chief azure an Irish harp gold stringed argent. [Confirmed by Carney, Ulster, c. 1690.]

[The modern coat remains in use, but through the disestablishment of the Irish Church it is really extinct and its present use is illegal.]
