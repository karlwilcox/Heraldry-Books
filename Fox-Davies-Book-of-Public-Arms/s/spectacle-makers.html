SPECTACLE-MAKERS, The Worshipful Company of, London. (Incorporated i6th INlay 1629.) Has no arms. Uses indifferently two spurious coats {a) azure, three pairs of spectacles, or {b) azure, a pair of compasses e-xpanded chevronwise between two pairs of spectacles in chief and a terrestrial globe on a stand in base, all argent. Crest — Two arms counter-embowed, vested (? azure) semee of mullets argent, cuffed argent, holding in the hands proper a serpent biting its tail in a circle, and within the same the sun in his splendour. Motto — " A blessing to the aged."

[Both of these devices are equally without authority.]
