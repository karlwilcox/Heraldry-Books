WALLINGFORD (Berkshire). Has no armorial bearings. The seal represents (according to the " General Armory ") a man on horseback at full speed armed cap-a-pie, and bearing on his left arm his shield, charged with the arms of France and England quarterly, on his helmet a cap of maintenance, thereon a lion statant guardant ducally crowned, his dexter arm extended and holding a sword erect, the pommel whereof is fastened to a chain which passes from the gorget : the horse fully caparisoned.
